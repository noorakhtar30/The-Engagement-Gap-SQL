# 📊 The Engagement Gap

## Investigating Why Visibility Fails to Translate into Audience Interaction

The Engagement Gap is an end-to-end SQL portfolio project that explores a critical question faced by content creators and marketers:

> Why do some posts achieve massive reach but fail to generate meaningful audience engagement?

Using MySQL, this project analyzes social media content performance across platforms, creators, audiences, and sponsorships to uncover the factors influencing audience interaction.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

High visibility does not always guarantee audience engagement.

Brands and creators often invest heavily in generating views and impressions, yet many campaigns fail to convert attention into meaningful interactions such as likes, comments, and shares.

This project investigates the "engagement gap" by identifying patterns associated with:

* High-reach but low-engagement content
* Low-reach but highly engaging posts
* Creator performance differences
* Sponsorship effectiveness
* Audience behavior across demographics
* Platform-specific engagement trends

## 🔍 Key Insights

* Visibility alone does not guarantee audience interaction.
* Several low-reach posts achieved exceptionally high engagement rates.
* Creator size did not consistently translate into stronger audience engagement.
* Sponsored posts varied significantly in their ability to convert views into interactions.
* Certain platforms generated high reach but exhibited larger engagement gaps.
* Audience demographics influenced engagement behavior across content categories.
